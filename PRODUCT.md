# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Singapore homeowners — primarily HDB flat owners and condo residents aged 25–45 — who are furnishing or refurnishing their homes. They're price-aware, have limited floor space, and want confidence that a piece will fit before committing. They shop online but worry about scale and fit in their actual room.

## Product Purpose

Avior Living is a headless Shopify storefront selling factory-direct furniture to Singapore homes. It cuts the retailer markup (typically 38–62%) and includes white-glove delivery, full assembly, and packaging disposal at no extra cost. Success means a visitor who was on the fence about a piece — unsure if it fits, unsure if it's worth the price — leaves with an order confirmed. The Room Planner is the key trust-builder: it lets buyers visualise furniture in their actual floor plan before buying.

## Brand Personality

Honest, precise, confident. Not aspirational-luxury, not bargain-basement. The brand says: "we know what the middleman charges; we don't charge it."

## Anti-references

- Generic Lazada/Shopee product-grid feel — no cheap e-commerce visual language
- Over-styled luxury lifestyle brands (Restoration Hardware, CB2) — Avior's story is value, not status
- Any site that leads with heavily filtered lifestyle photography and vague brand copy
- SaaS-cream landing pages with icon grids and tracked uppercase eyebrows everywhere

## Design Principles

1. **Show the savings, don't just claim them** — price comparisons and the Room Planner demo are the page's most important elements; give them space and credibility.
2. **Precision over polish** — clean lines, accurate information, and honest dimensions signal the same trustworthiness as the product itself.
3. **Local context matters** — Singapore HDB dimensions, mm-based floor plans, and delivery logistics should feel native, not translated from a generic global template.
4. **Demonstrate, don't describe** — the Room Planner section should show the tool working, not explain it in bullet points.
5. **Confidence by default** — the brand tone is direct and assured; avoid hedging language and decorative elements that dilute the value message.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All video content is muted and decorative (no critical information in audio). Floor plan tool must be keyboard and touch accessible. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast on all backgrounds.
